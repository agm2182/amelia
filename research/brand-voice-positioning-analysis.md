# Cherri Brand Voice & Positioning

**Last Updated:** January 24, 2026

---

## The Core Positioning

**Cherri is for the girl who GETS IT.**

Not the "it-girl." Not the clinical health shopper. Not the sustainability virtue signaler. The girl who's been dealing with underwear that doesn't fit for years and is relieved someone finally made something that works.

### What Cherri IS

- **Fit-first:** Wide gusset, no lip slips, designed for how you're actually built
- **Relatable:** Humor from shared experience ("I started this because MY underwear sucked")
- **Direct:** Talks about vulvas, flaps, and coochies without being clinical or weird about it
- **Female-founded:** Founder who HAD the problem, not designing for someone else

### What Cherri is NOT

- **"It-girl" aspirational** (that's Underdays, Fruity Booty)
- **Clinical health brand** (that's Huha, ODDOBODY)
- **Sustainability-first** (that's Fruity Booty with deadstock)
- **Body positivity lectures** (that's millennial brands)

## The Brand Voice

### Voice Characteristics

| Trait | What It Sounds Like | What It Doesn't Sound Like |
|-------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **Cheeky** | "Christmas tree? What about Christmas bush?" | "Celebrate your body this holiday season!" |
| **Direct** | "Your flaps don't fall out of your underwear anymore" | "Our patented gusset technology provides enhanced coverage" |
| **Relatable** | "Ya'll are doing the deed at night??" | "When you're ready for intimacy..." |
| **Playful** | "vajussy," "p$$y," "fruitcake" | "vagina," "vulva" (fine to use, but mix with playful) |
| **Not try-hard** | Casual delivery, not performing | Overly enthusiastic, exclamation marks everywhere |
| **Problem-aware** | "thong that doesn't slice my lil fruitcake in half" | "Experience comfort like never before" |

### What Works on TikTok (760K followers)

**Top performing content themes:**

1. **Origin story** - "I started an underwear company so your flaps don't fall out of your underwear anymore" (24.7M views)
2. **Vulva humor** - "we're outies" (14.9M views) - normalizes anatomy, creates community
3. **Relatable problems** - "All I want for Xmas is a thong that doesn't slice my lil fruitcake in half"
4. **Gift context** - "POV you bought your friend the viral wide gusset undies"
5. **Confidence** - "Pop your p$$y bc Cherri is on sale"

**Language that works:**
- "Christmas bush," "vajussy," "lip slips," "fruitcake," "flaps"
- Holiday/seasonal hooks
- Dating, sex, body humor
- Self-deprecating jokes

### Platform Differences

| Platform | Content Style | Voice Style |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **TikTok** | Raw, humor-first, trending sounds | Irreverent, direct, vulva jokes |
| **Instagram** | Curated grid, editorial shots, lifestyle | Still cheeky, but cooler/shorter captions |

Same brand, different energy. TikTok is the friend who makes you laugh. Instagram is her curated photo dump.

## Messaging Hierarchy

### Primary Message (Lead With This)

**"Underwear that actually fits"**

- Wide gusset = no lip slips
- Designed for how you're actually built
- Solves a problem no one else talks about

### Supporting Message

**"Made by someone who gets it"**

- Female founder who HAD the problem
- Humor from shared experience
- Not designing for someone else (looking at you, male founder competitors)

### Tertiary Message (Show, Don't Tell)

**Body diversity and inclusivity**

- XS-4X sizing
- Show diverse bodies in imagery, don't lecture about it
- Artsy/editorial treatment, not clinical

### Health as Bonus (Don't Lead With This)

**Breathable, non-toxic materials**

- Supima cotton modal blend
- Non-toxic dyes
- Prevents irritation and infections

Health is a BONUS, not the selling point. Deliver casually, not clinically. "Also breathable and good for your coochie" not "Designed to support vaginal microbiome health."

## What NOT to Do

### Avoid These

1. **Body positivity lectures** - "Love yourself!" "You're beautiful!" "Real women have curves!"
2. **Clinical language** - "Our patented technology provides..." "Designed by gynecologists..."
3. **Over-explanation** - "Our wide gusset technology measures 4 inches and provides..."
4. **Empowerment cringe** - "Boss babe," "Queen," "Slay" (unless ironic)
5. **Try-hard humor** - Forced jokes, overused memes, exclamation marks everywhere
6. **Hiding the founder** - Your face and story built 760K followers. Don't disappear behind aesthetics.

### The Millennial Body Positivity Trap

Millennial brands did body positivity like this:
> "Every body is beautiful! We celebrate ALL women! Love yourself, queen!"

GenZ and Elder GenZ are allergic to this. It feels performative, corporate, and preachy.

Cherri does it like this:
> [Shows diverse bodies in imagery]
> "wide gusset. no lip slips."

Show, don't tell. Confidence is assumed, not preached.

## Segment-Specific Messaging

### Core Audience (25-34) - 50% of audience

**Who she is:** Has dealt with "front wedgies" for years. Thought it was just her body. Previous underwear was "okay" but not great.

**Lead with:** The origin story. "No lip slips." The "finally" moment.

**Channel:** TikTok → TikTok Shop

### Active Lifestyle (Nurses, gym-goers, horseback riders) - 15%

**Who she is:** Underwear shifts during 12-hour shifts or workouts. Constantly adjusting.

**Lead with:** Function. "Stays put during your 12-hour shift." "Set it and forget it."

**Channel:** Search/problem → TikTok → Website

### Plus-Size Shoppers - 15%

**Who she is:** Frustrated by brands that just scale up small patterns. Needs underwear designed for her body.

**Lead with:** "Designed for real bodies, not sized up." Show diverse bodies artistically.

**Channel:** TikTok/Reddit → Website

### Perimenopausal / Elder Millennials (35-44) - 18%

**Who she is:** Body changing, old underwear doesn't fit. Done with fast fashion quality.

**Lead with:** Quality materials. Comfort over trend. Worth the $18.

**Channel:** Instagram → Website

### Parade Refugees (22-30) - 10%

**Who she is:** Was loyal to Parade, now needs a replacement. Values sustainability, inclusivity, fun.

**Lead with:** Similar values + functional differentiation. "Your new go-to."

**Channel:** TikTok → TikTok Shop

## Instagram Evolution

The Instagram aesthetic is evolving toward "Pinterest-y cool girl" while keeping the relatable voice.

### Current → Target State

| Element | Now | Target |
|---------|-----|--------|
| Grid | Mixed, spontaneous | Intentional, editorial |
| Product shots | Functional | Artsy, magazine-quality |
| Colors | Natural | Muted, film-like |
| Lifestyle | Casual | Styled, aspirational but not "it-girl" |
| Memes | Text screenshots | Designed, on-brand, Pinterest-saveable |

### The Key Tension

TikTok = raw, bedroom-selfie, talking-to-camera humor
Instagram = curated, editorial, Pinterest-y

**This isn't a problem.** The 25-34 demo expects different content on different platforms. The key is brand recognition across both.

**The solution:** Instagram should be "cool girl who also makes coochie jokes" - not "fashion brand that sometimes reposts TikToks."

## The Summary

Cherri is for the girl who GETS IT.

Not the it-girl. Not the health-obsessed. Not the sustainability warrior. The girl who's been dealing with underwear that doesn't fit and is relieved someone finally understood.

Lead with fit. Support with humor. Show (don't tell) the body diversity. Deliver health as a bonus, not a promise.

The 760K TikTok followers came from being relatable, not aspirational. Don't lose that trying to be "fashiony."
