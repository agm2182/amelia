# Parade Underwear Post-Mortem

**Last Updated:** January 2026

## Executive Summary

Parade, the Gen Z-focused DTC underwear brand founded in 2019, shut down permanently on October 28, 2025, after six years. Despite raising $56 million, achieving a peak valuation of $200 million, and building a cult following, the company was sold to Ariela & Associates in 2023 for a fraction of its valuation (wiping out investors), then limped along for two more years before closing.

## 3. Target Market & Positioning

**Target:** Gen Z consumers (18-27)

**Positioning:**
- Anti-Victoria's Secret alternative
- Core: body positivity, inclusivity, sustainability
- Sizing: XS to 3XL (later 5XL)
- Price: $8-$14 underwear; $48-$62 bras
- Gender-inclusive marketing with diverse representation

**Differentiation:**
- Unretouched campaign imagery
- Sustainability (recycled materials, Second Life recycling program)
- Community: 75,000+ brand ambassadors

## 4. What Worked Initially

**Ambassador Program Innovation:**
- 75,000+ mostly unpaid ambassadors
- Targeted micro-influencers over celebrities
- Sent free products to 6,000+ people in first year
- Created community and Instagram fame for everyday consumers

**Viral Launch:**
- Founder's Google Docs manifesto against Victoria's Secret went viral
- 70,000+ waitlist signups before launch
- Strong organic social presence

**Early Metrics (2019-2020):**
- 500,000+ pairs in first year
- $10M revenue by end of 2020
- 500%+ revenue growth during 2020
- 100,000+ customers by year one

**Cultural Timing:**
- Victoria's Secret declining
- Body positivity wave
- Pandemic e-commerce boom

## 5. What Went Wrong

### A. Growth vs. Profitability Conflict

Fundamental tension between founder Cami Tellez and investors. Tellez wanted profitability; major investors (Maveron, Stripes) pushed growth-first strategy. Neither agreed, resulting in inconsistent execution.

### B. Unsustainable Unit Economics

**Marketing Spend Spiral:**
- 2020: 30% of revenue on marketing
- 2021: 60% of revenue on marketing
- 2022: 40% after cost-cutting

**Burn Rate:**
- 2022: $21 million loss on $49 million sales
- 2023: On track for $14 million burn

### C. Rising Customer Acquisition Costs

Cheap social media era ended. Apple's iOS privacy changes and competition raised CAC dramatically.

### D. Fierce Competition

Well-funded rivals emerged:
- **Savage X Fenty** (2018) - Rihanna
- **Skims** (2019) - Kim Kardashian
- **Yitty** (2022) - Lizzo
- Plus: ThirdLove, Adore Me, Cuup, Lively, Aerie

### E. Late Wholesale Expansion

Admitted launching wholesale later than peers. Wholesale margins often exceed DTC.

### F. Inventory Mismanagement

Overspent on inventory, tying up needed capital.

### G. Failed Fundraising 2023

Higher interest rates + poor DTC IPO performance (Allbirds). Approached investors, all rejected, forcing fire-sale.

## 6. Customer Feedback Before Shutdown

### Pre-Acquisition (2019-2023):
- **Sizing inconsistency:** Changed between collections
- **Style creep:** Boyshorts rode up, became thong-like
- **Durability:** Waistbands twisted, mesh frayed, colors faded
- **Bralette sizing:** Cups too small
- **Email spam:** Excessive marketing

### Post-Acquisition (2023-2025):
- **Quality decline:** Products fell apart after one wash
- **Price increases:** Closeout higher than original prices
- **Reduced inclusivity:** Abandoned core values
- **Poor customer service:** No responses, phones inactive
- **Reduced size range:** Size-inclusive mission deprioritized

**Representative Quote:** "So sad to see you guys go, can't help but feel like the pivot away from what made yall stand out is what did you in."

## 8. Lessons for Cherri

### Strategic Lessons:

1. **Align on Strategy Early**
   Resolve growth vs. profitability debate with investors before taking money. Parade's board never agreed.

2. **Control Marketing Spend**
   60% of revenue on marketing is unsustainable. Set CAC guardrails.

3. **Build Wholesale Early**
   3/4 of DTC brands never reach $1M. Wholesale margins often exceed DTC.

4. **Prepare for Competition**
   Celebrity-backed competitors can emerge overnight. Build defensible advantages.

5. **Sustainable Unit Economics First**
   Profitability at small scale predicts profitability at large scale.

6. **Quality is Non-Negotiable**
   Underwear is replenishment. If products fail, customers won't return.

### Operational Lessons:

7. **Conservative Inventory**
   Overspending ties up capital.

8. **Diversify Acquisition Channels**
   Over-reliance on social creates existential risk.

9. **Maintain Values Post-Acquisition**
   Parade's new owners abandoned inclusivity/quality, accelerating defection.

10. **Plan for Capital Market Changes**
    Cheap capital era ended. Build businesses that survive without constant fundraising.

### Founder Reflection:

Tellez concluded the era for fast-scaling, market-share-grabbing startups is behind us. Next-generation brands will be capitalized differently.
